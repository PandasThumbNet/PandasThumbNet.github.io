alan bates · 1 May 2012

Further to Frank J and Flint: Google is your friend. Try googling North Menan Butte. On the first page there is an item from Oregon State Uni: http://volcano.oregonstate.edu/vwdocs/volc_images/north_america/menan_buttes.html with more photos and a bit of backgropund. The buttes are on the Snake River Plain. To quote the page:
The Menan Buttes formed when a dike intruded into a shallow aquifer. The water turned to steam and explosively fragmented the basaltic magma. The cones are late Pleistocene in age.
According to Wiki (?!) the North and South buttes are late Pleistocene, 10,000 YBP (IIRC, dated back from 1950). This is supported by: http://www.blm.gov/id/st/en/fo/upper_snake/recreation_sites_/north_menan_butte.html The mixing of basaltic magma and water would have caused a violent phraetic explosion. The 2 cones, North and South, are amongst the largest in the world. More info. at: http://earthobservatory.nasa.gov/IOTD/view.php?id=48097 also from the first page in the Google search. Again, this supports the figure of 10,000YBP.
