Slab avalanche

Posted 21 December 2009 by Matt Young

This avalanche was only a meter or 2 high and occurred in what appeared to be a reservoir for snow-making at Eldora Mountain Resort, hence the plastic underlay. The slope is on the leeward side of the "mountain"; note the cornices to the left of the avalanche.

Paul Burnett · 21 December 2009

Matt Young said: I thought of it as an avalanche in microcosm. Why is it not?
Primarily because it didn't go anywhere. According to Wikipedia (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avalanche): "An avalanche is a rapid flow of snow down a slope" Avalanches have common elements including "...a slide path along which the avalanche flows, a run out where the avalanche comes to rest, and a debris deposit which is the accumulated mass of the avalanched snow once it has come to rest." That large flat structure in the picture is not a slide track - its just the now-unattached surface of the snow pack - you can see still-attached similar surfaces further to the left.
