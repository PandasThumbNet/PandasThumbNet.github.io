Alan B · 6 October 2009

A beautiful, atmospheric, photograph typical of some of the mornings we are having here (England, West Midlands). Sorbus aucuparia is far better known in the UK as "Mountain Ash" or less commonly as "Rowan". I note the only reference to "wiggen tree" given in Wiki is to a couple of 19th century articles on witchcraft. I presume the common name comes because the leaves have a resemblance to ash. The mountain will come from its ecology:
"Rowan is very tolerant of cold and is often found at high altitude on mountains; in the UK it occurs at up to 1 000 m altitude, higher than any other tree, and in France up to 2 000 m."
(Wiki) Interesting article from the Royal Botanic Gardens (Kew): http://apps.kew.org/trees/?page_id=125
