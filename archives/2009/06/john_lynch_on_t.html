I say may have, because if you back and read the posts in context, it is pretty clear that my point was that many areas that people assume they can just throw up their hands and declare the matter one of opinion, are not. They yield to systematic investigation because they do have properties that exist outside of my perception or thoughts about them. I didn't mean that science tried to investigage idiosyncratic issues like who has a funnier mustache. You could however investigate the effectiveness of humor. The sentence "9-12 million people were systematically murdered by the Nazis" is a conclusion drawn from many documents. As I noted above, this conclusion is not in dispute. What is in dispute is when, where, how and why the policy emerged. And that question too should not be left to opinion, but to systematic investigation.
