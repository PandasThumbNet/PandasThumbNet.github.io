The Smithsonian also displays tons of privately owned objects (most if not all of the museum’s gems and jewelry, for example), and in those cases too the donors typically don’t get much more than their names or “from the estate of…” on a small card. I would certainly be upset if the museum treated the Koch brothers differently and gave them more signage/more content control than they give to other donors. The museum isn’t going to be ‘tainted’ any more than they were by the thousands of other private donations they’ve already taken, the signage for which most visitors blithely and completely ignore as they view the exhibits.
