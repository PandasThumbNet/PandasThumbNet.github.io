As Bugs Bunny would say, "Ehhhh.... could be!" The Hebrew word ×Ö·×Ö·×¨Ö°×Ö¹Ö¼Ö£× ("hahargol"), translated "beetle" by the KJV, occurs only once in the OT, so its meaning is a guess from context. It possibly might mean "beetle", but more likely it means "cricket" or some specific species of cicada. The reason that these translations are more likely is because "beetle" is not consonant with the general rule given in v 21: "those that have legs jointed above their feet for leaping in the air".
