I think you are mistaking stupidity, which would indicate he was incapable of comprehending your points, and *willful* ignorance - where he is capable of understanding but chooses not to.

This implies a notion of libertarian fee will that is highly suspect. If he "chooses" not to understand, it's not clear that there's any sense in which he could choose to understand. I would argue that either exposure to the material induces understanding in him or it doesn't. OTOH, he might understand but feign otherwise, but that should be detectable with careful enough attention to his behavior. I think Norm is arguing that such an analysis suggests that his lack of understanding is genuine. Also, there seems to be the assumption that self-induced failure to understand is smart if it leads to monetary gain, but it's not self-evident that monetary gain is preferable to understanding. In my experience, people who come to understand things are generally glad they did, even if it results in losing something they cherished (a belief in eternal life or being created for a purpose, for instance).
