Pope Benedict, Theistic Evolutionist

Posted 11 April 2007 by Wesley R. Elsberry

This news article reports on a topic that tugs at antievolutionist heartstrings: would the Pope, leader of the Catholic church, throw in with them, joining them in the "intelligent design" big tent? The answer, at least according to this news report, is "No". Pope Benedict is reported to adopt theistic evolution, the idea that God's method of creation is what science has discovered concerning evolutionary biology. And we know from William Dembski that "intelligent design theorists" are no friends of theistic evolution. A lot of the coverage has concentrated on Benedict's stance against atheism, which seems to me to be about as newsworthy as taking up the question, "Is the Pope Catholic?" Well, yes, it seems that he is.

I personally find the processes of evolution awesome ones (and I find the physical evolution of the universe from the big bang to the formation of later-generation stars and planets just as fantastic as biological evolution). Since I have started to study these things more deeply, the concept of God has become for me even greater than it already was.
