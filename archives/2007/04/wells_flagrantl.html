PZ Myers · 4 April 2007

You are talking about different things.

— realpc
No, we're talking about the same thing. There is a canonical arrangement of genes that is inferred from the arrangements in extant species, that is evidence for common descent; flies and people both carry versions of a similar Hox arrangement, but the fly copy is more degraded. There is also evidence of variation that supports a pattern of evolutionary change. Wells completely misstated the evidence of the paper. It shows evidence for both common descent of a level of genetic organization shared between invertebrates and vertebrates, and it shows the evolutionary relationships within one clade at a reasonably high level of detail.
