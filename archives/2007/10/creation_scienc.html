Uh, wolfwalker, humor is often relevant to key issues. The more laughs, the more relevant. Doesn't necessarily point to truisms, but more to areas of controversy. Some would conclude that those areas are demeaned by the humor in a way that supports the attacking position. That might be true where the humorist goes on with more one-liners supporting the inanity proffered, and that these garner laughs as well. It's been shown that points made with humor are more readily accepted than those made otherwise, which was demonstrated in the early days of TV, when the 'serious sells' ("Anacin is like a doctor's prescription, that is ...") gave way to 'Charlie the Tuna' et al. Humor may insult, it may obfuscate, but often, by lowering the bar it may actually help to further a point of view.

That said, maybe AiG should post a link to Roy's creationism video.
