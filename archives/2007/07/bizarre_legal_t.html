Bizarre Legal Thinking on Evolution and Creationism

Posted 30 July 2007 by Ed Brayton

I came across a reference to a law review note from last year in the Chapman Law Review. The note was by Stephen Trask, then a student at William Mitchell College of Law and, unsurprisingly, a graduate of Liberty University. It was entitled, Evolution, Science, and Ideology: Why the Establishment Clause Requires Neutrality in Science Classes. Since Chapman is Sandefur's alma mater, I emailed him to see if he'd seen it and he said no, but he found it and sent me a copy of it. He described it as a "giant, steaming pile of crap"; he was being generous. He's going to post a full fisking of the article soon, but I wanted to focus on one particular claim Trask makes.
