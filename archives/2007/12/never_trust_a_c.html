Posted 13 December 2007 by PZ Myers

It does not speak well of the Discovery Institute that they had to cobble together quote-mines from the email to try and make a non-case for a non-issue.

I guess that most of the commenters (myself included) have not had direct contact with people involved in the decision over Gonzalez's tenure application. (2) Until all of the facts are in the public domain, we are all forming judgements on a basis of limited (or at least incomplete) information, with the exception of those who were actually involved in the decision. The key messages before the emails came out seemed to me to be along these lines: (1) Gonzalez was not granted tenure because he was not doing good science, not because he was an ID proponent. (2) Academics who publicly support antiscience (including Gonzalez) should not be granted tenure in science departments. (3) Gonzalez's public support of ID raised serious doubts about his ability to recognise good science.
