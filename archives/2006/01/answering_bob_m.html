Answering Bob Murphy on Objections to ID

Posted 25 January 2006 by Ed Brayton

Bob Murphy, an economist, has an article at the thoroughly loathsome LewRockwell.com (please don't try and tell me the people who write for that site are libertarians; that collection of southern nationalists and whackos is anything but libertarian) about what he terms "typical objections to intelligent design." Much like Orson Scott Card's article, however, this one relies upon the old tactic of beating up straw men. Rather than engaging the strongest objections to ID he only engages the easy ones, the clumsy attacks on the character of ID advocates rather than the serious and substantive criticism of the validity of ID itself. Let's take a look at them one at a time. Continue Reading at Dispatches from the Culture Wars. Comments may be left there.
