Smithsonian's Human Origins Initiative

Posted 12 March 2010 by Richard B. Hoppe

The Smithsonian Institution has launched a new web site focused on human origins. It includes a good deal of material on the evidence (behavior, fossils, genetics, and dating) and Smithsonian's research projects, along with what looks like a very useful set of education resources including lesson plans for teachers, a teachers forum, and student resources including an interactive mystery skull interactive exercise (I had trouble with that in Chrome but not in Firefox; apparently there's a Flash glitch in the interaction of the site with Chrome). And just to stir the pot a little, the page on the Broader Social Impacts Committee will provide some fuel to the accommodationist/hardliner feud. In particular, notice who is not represented on it. At any rate, I strongly commend the site to your attention. Hat tip to ASA Voices.
