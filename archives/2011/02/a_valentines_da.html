I always wanted to treat my sweetie to a candlelit dinner finished off with a dramatic reading of Langdon Smith's poem "Evolution" from circa 1895 (the one that begins with "When you were a tadpole and I was a fish"). It's nominally about reincarnation, following two lovers over millions of years, but Martin Gardner pointed out in one of his collections of poetry that "if you are not sympathetic to that doctrine you can take it as a whimsical metaphor for the continuity of life."

"I was thewed like an Aurochs bull
