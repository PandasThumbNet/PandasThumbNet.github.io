Call for links for the Tangled Bank

Posted 26 November 2005 by PZ Myers

Another Tangled Bank is coming up on Wednesday, 30 November, at Dogged Blog. Send those links in to the host or me soon.

If you're wondering what the Tangled Bank is, Mike Bergin has written a summary of weblog carnivals. It's basically a mechanism to spread the attention around, and direct readers away from a few well-trafficked weblogs to other deserving sites, usually with a theme—the Tangled Bank is a one-stop shop to find lots of weblogs that write about science, for instance.

Incidentally, if you had to have faith in "Darwinism" you're better off without it. The entire point of the body of knowledge called science (which includes the field more properly known as evolutionary biology*) is that you don't have to have faith in it. It's a measured, unbiased attempt to draw the best possible conclusions from the available data, and to acquire the most accurate possible data to challenge these conclusions. For this reason, science tends to provide the most accurate portrayal of events, because if someone has a better portrayal then science adopts it. * That's right, there's no such thing as Darwinism in science. Science has no infallible doctrines, just the current best guess. Of course, this can look very like doctrine, for sufficiently strong value of "best".
