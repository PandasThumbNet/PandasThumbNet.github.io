The "who made God?" retort follows from an argument something like this:

IDist: All objects with property X must be designed (complexity or whatever).

Interlocuter: The designer you propose then must also have property X. Ergo, it must've been designed too. Ditto for its designer and on down the line.

The IDist, likely believing his designer is God, does not think it has a designer. Ergo, the attempt is to put the IDist in a bind. Some will say it's different because "the designer" is supernatural. Some will argue "the designer" is responsible for its own nature. And so on. But it's not a childish response. It's a sharp, albiet obvious objection.
