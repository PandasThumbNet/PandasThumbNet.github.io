fusilier · 15 April 2016

Henry J said: I've heard that insects that large could have trouble breathing, but I guess this guy's thin enough that all body parts are close enough to the skin to get oxygen supply.
Insects have a complex arrangement of channels, leading directly from openings ("spiracles")in the cuticle to all parts of the body. Further, the body pumps air through the channels by a series of movements. If you ever watch a butterfly at a flower, you can see this happening. So oxygen is actively transported, not just moving by diffusion. The wings don't have living cells in them, once they are fully unfolded, so there's no need to get oxygen or nutrients to them. fusilier, wearing his old Insect Taxonomist hat, from 40 years ago James 2:24
